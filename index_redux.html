Your Employment

Commencing your Employment

Your Contract

Your contract will contain all the terms & conditions of your employment, unless it otherwise refers to an enterprise agreement. Any temporary or permanent changes to your T&C’s will be confirmed with a letter stating the changes.

Evidence of Qualifications

Any qualifications or tickets you supplied as a condition of your employment will be electronically kept on your employee record and will be provided to clients when required to attain approval for access to project sites.

Pre-employment Medical

If you were required to complete a pre-employment medical prior to starting with MHPS, the completed medical forms will be kept on your employee record. The medical may be provided to clients to attain approval for access to project sites. Medicals will be valid for two years.

Your Qualifying Period

All permanent employees are subject to a 6 month qualifying period. This allows both the employee and the business to assess suitability, fit and competency. Ongoing employment will be confirmed following an end-of-period performance review.

Supply of ICT Equipment

Your manager is responsible for arranging your IT hardware and system access prior to you starting. Any IT issues should be logged on the IT Support site.

Access to Information

Our Integrated Management System is the central repository of MHPS policies, procedures, operating manuals and other useful internal information and links.

Ending your Employment

Resignation Requirements

If you wish to resign or retire from your position, consult your employment contract or the HR department to understand your notice period obligations.
Your manager will require a written resignation, either in the format of a letter or an email.

Exit Questionnaire

Once the HR department receives your resignation, they will send you a link to an online exit questionnaire. Please take the time to complete the questionnaire, as this will assist us in identifying areas of concern and also in highlighting what you enjoyed in your time with MHPS.

Return of Company Equipment

You must arrange to return MHPS property immediately when requested and upon termination of your employment.
This includes IT equipment and may include some PPE items.

Final Pay

You are likely to receive your final pay, including any unused annual leave entitlements, within a day or two of your last official day with the company.

Rehire

Former employees who left MHPS Plant Services in good standing and were classified as eligible for rehire may be considered for re-employment. An application must be submitted to the Human Resource department, and the applicant must meet all minimum qualifications and requirements of the position.

Company History

>> >> >>


MHPS is an engineering and construction company. Our specialisation is the design, supply, construction, maintenance and overhaul of boilers, boiler auxiliaries and other energy and mechanical plant. We provide our products and services to the Power and Process Industries in Australia, Oceania and South East Asia.

The knowledge, specialised skills, and experience from its long association with boiler plant has additionally made MHPS a leading provider of overhaul and maintenance services to owners of boiler and other plant throughout Australia.

In 1933, Melbourne engineer and businessman Mr. Jim Dodds obtained a license agreement with Riley Stoker Inc of USA and established Riley Dodds (Australia) Ltd (RDA) for the purpose of designing and building boilers for Australian and New Zealand requirements.

The company prospered and continued in operation for almost forty years. In August 1990 Babcock-Hitachi K.K. (BHK) of Japan purchased the majority shareholding from Clyde Industries and Clyde Babcock-Hitachi (Australia) Pty Ltd (CBH) was established, jointly owned by BHK and Clyde Industries.

In October 2014 BHK merged to become Mitsubishi Hitachi Power Systems (MHPS) and CBH became Mitsubishi Hitachi Power Systems – Plant Services.

Employee Behaviour Expectations


MHPS has numerous policies in place which outline the standard of behaviour expected of MHPS employees.

They are designed to assist employees to understand their responsibilities and obligations and to provide guidance on expected behaviour in the workplace. These policies do not seek to encompass all possible scenarios arising in employment with MHPS, however it provides a set of principles to guide staff on acceptable and unacceptable behaviour.

All Policies can be found in the Policy & Procedures tab in the navigation bar above.

Code of Conduct

The MHI Code of Conduct outlines the standard of behaviour expected of MHPS employees. It provides a core set of principles to guide staff on acceptable and unacceptable behaviour.

  • Respect human rights, and do not discriminate on any basis or engage in any form of harassment
  • Maintain a high regard for environmental considerations in conducting our business operations, and ensure that our business is conducted in an environmentally sustainable manner, and comply with treaties, laws and regulations concerning the environment
  • Promote fair business practices and comply with trade rules, regulations, and internal corporate rules and policies
  • Comply with the rules and regulations of international trade
  • Protect and properly use confidential and proprietary information, protect the rights of the Company and respect the rights of others
  • Do not engage in insider trading
  • Avoid conflicts of interest with the Company; maintain a distinction between corporate and private business
  • Record and report accounting and financial information timely and accurately
  • Maintain proper legal and ethical standards with respect to gifts and entertainment
  • Resolutely oppose any organisation, group or individual engaged in unlawful activities and do not provide money or other types of economic benefits to them
  • Promptly report to or consult the executive leadership team, the Group Compliance Officers or the relevant departments upon discovering or committing any violations of this "Code of Conduct"

Late for Work

Any absence or late arrival must be personally reported to your supervisor or Manager prior to your normal starting time or as soon as possible. If you are unable to do this personally, you are requested to ask someone to telephone on your behalf. Any lateness or absence should preferably be reported with a phone call rather than other forms of communication such as SMS or email.

Disciplinary Issues

The Disciplinary Policy sets out the procedures and guidelines for:

  • Key principles for dealing with employee counselling and disciplinary procedures
  • Work performance counselling
  • Disciplinary procedures, including the handling of unacceptable conduct

Issues related to Employee work performance or conduct will normally be addressed by the employer through a procedure that commences with counselling, before proceeding to a formal and structured discipline procedure.

Workplace Bullying

The Workplace Bullying Prevention Policy educates employees on the nature and effects of bullying; the consequences of being involved in any form of bullying, directly or indirectly, and their responsibilities in preventing and managing such incidents.

MHPS requires all employees to behave responsibly by complying with this policy, to not tolerate unacceptable behaviour, to maintain privacy during investigations and to immediately report incidents of workplace bullying.

Discrimination & Harassment

The Anti-discrimination & Harrassment Policy outlines MHPS’s expectations of employees and helps employees to understand their legal obligations in relation to discrimination, harassment, vilification and victimisation within the workplace.

Grievances

The Grievance Policy & Procedure provides information and advice on what a grievance is; appropriate steps employees can take before making a grievance; what employees should do if they have a grievance and what will happen if an employee raises a formal complaint.

Matters covered in this policy include, but are not limited to:

  • Discrimination, victimisation or vilification
  • Workplace bullying or harassment
  • Breaches of any MHPS policy
  • An unfair or unreasonable conduct of an employee, manager or client that adversely affects an employee
  • An unfair or unreasonable decision by MHPS that adversely affects an employee

All grievances will be managed in a way which is open, transparent and fair to all parties involved. They will be treated seriously and confidentially and will be the subject of a thorough investigation to ensure an appropriate and timely resolution.

Use of Social Media


All staff are expected at all times to protect the privacy, confidentiality, and interests of our company and our services, employees, partners, customers, and competitors.

  • Only the persons approved by the HR Manager or General Manager of Business Development are permitted to post material on a social media website in our name and on our behalf.
  • Do not upload, post, forward or post a link to any abusive, obscene, discriminatory, harassing, derogatory or defamatory content.
  • Never disclose commercially sensitive, anti-competitive, private or confidential information. If you are unsure whether the information you wish to share falls within one of these categories, you should discuss this with your immediate supervisor.

Working Arrangements

Flexibility

MHPS supports flexible working arrangements where appropriate and viable – discuss possibilities with your manager.

Working from Home

MHPS has no current Working from Home policy, but if required, discuss with your manager the options for temporary working from home arrangements.

Rostered Days Off

Permanent salaried employees can participate in the RDO scheme – If employees adopt a 40 hour working week, they will be eligible to take a pre-approved day off every four weeks. Details of the RDO Policy can be found on the IMS. Leave requests need to be pre-approved by your manager.

Meal Breaks

You are entitled to take an unpaid lunch break of 30 minutes. You are not required to work for more than five hours without a meal break. You are responsible for organising your break times in consultation with your manager or the client

Allowances & Overtime

Overtime is work which is performed at the direction of your manager and which is in excess of your contracted hours of work. Eligibility and/or payment for overtime are different depending on your employment conditions. Refer to your Employment Contract, Enterprise Agreement or Manager for further details. All overtime requires pre-approval from your Manager.

Taking Leave


All leae requests must be submitted via Kronos

Annual Leave

Full-time salaried employees are entitled to four weeks leave per annum. Annual leave needs to be pre-approved by your manager.

You may be directed to take Annual Leave if you have accumulated more than eight weeks’ of leave.

The MHPS offices generally close down over the Christmas period (2-3 weeks), and you may be required to take part or all of your accrued Annual Leave for the duration of the close down.
If you do not have sufficient accrued Annual Leave then you may be required to take unpaid leave over the period of the close down.

Sick/Carer’s Leave

Full-time salaried employees are entitled to ten days’ personal/carer’s leave per annum.

Additional unpaid carer’s leave of up to two 2 days’ per occasion may be taken once all paid carer’s leave has been exhausted.

Given the generally unplanned nature of personal/carer’s leave, you may submit a leave application form on your return to work. However, you must inform your manager of your intention to take leave and your expected return date as soon as possible on the day of the absence.

Your manager may request a medical certificate if you have two or more days of personal/carer’s Leave within a short period of time.

Long Service Leave

Service is calculated from the date you join the company as a permanent employee. Queensland-based salaried employees are entitled to paid long service leave after ten years of continuous service – other states may have different eligibility criteria and limits for long service leave.

Public Holidays

Public Holidays are observed as applicable to your work location. Compensation for public holidays worked may take the form of payment for hours worked or time off in lieu.

Compassionate Leave

Should an immediate family member sustain a personal injury or illness that poses a serious threat to their life, or should an immediate family member pass away, permanent employees will be entitled to two days paid compassionate leave.

Parental Leave & Extended Leave

Parental Leave Policy

Parental or adoption leave eligibility will be dependent on the employment type and length of service of an employee.

The employee must provide MHPS Plant Services with written notice of the intention to take parental leave at least ten weeks before the leave commences, or as soon is reasonably practicable.

Community Service Leave

You are entitled to Community Service leave in accordance with National Employment Standards.

Study Leave

Consult with your Manager should you require leave to complete any study you are undertaking.

Unpaid Leave

Unpaid leave may be granted at the discretion of the manager only once all annual leave entitlements are exhausted.
